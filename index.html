🏁 Race format, standards & rules

The format is identical at every race: 8 × 1 km run, each followed by one functional station, always in this order. Loads shown are yours for the selected division.

Judging & no-reps

• Wall balls: squat below parallel (hip crease under knee) and the ball must hit the target — miss either and it doesn't count.
• Burpee broad jump: chest and thighs to the floor, then jump forward; both feet must land past the marker.
• Sled push/pull: the whole sled must cross the line each length.
• Farmers carry: no dropping the bells between the lines; set-downs cost you.
• Sandbag lunges: the trailing knee must clearly touch the ground each rep; bag stays on your back/shoulders.
• Roxzone: the transition area between run and station — the clock never stops, so move with purpose. No outside help or pacing.

Good to know

• Judges can issue no-reps (redo the rep) or a Roxzone time penalty for standards not met.
• Divisions: Open, Pro (heavier loads), Doubles (share stations, run all 8 km each) and Relay.
• Wear comfortable closed-toe trainers; grip gloves are allowed and popular for the sleds and carry.

Race week

• Nothing new on race day — kit, shoes, food, pacing all rehearsed.
• Taper means taper: short, sharp, rested. Fitness is already banked.
• Go out conservative — the race starts at wall balls.
• Walk the venue, learn the Roxzone layout, carb up the day before, arrive early.
